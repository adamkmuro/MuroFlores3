Minor Infractions

How much could your law-breaking ways cost you if the NYPD decided to crack down?

Ivan Flores, Adam Muro

Living in New York City is stressful.

You’re paying too much to live in a box, stacked on top of boxes full of other people. You get up most mornings and cram yourself into another box, this one occasionally hot and smelly, that travels underground to take you to work.

Not to mention the all the rules. You accept that living in a populous city requires everyone to give up certain small freedoms to live harmoniously. But with 36,000 of New York’s finest breathing down your neck on every corner, it can feel like you’re always being watched, like they’re just waiting for you to put a toe out of line.

So sometimes you break the rules.

We’ve compiled a list of things most New Yorkers do on a weekly basis without thinking about it that are actually illegal. Previously, these low level offenses, known as “quality of life crimes,” could have landed you with a permanent criminal record.

But, since the New York City Council passed the Criminal Justice Reform Act in 2016, the majority of these offenses are now handled with simple civil summons’, also known as tickets.

That means you could still be on the hook for a fine. Take the quiz below to find out how much you could owe if you were caught, and scroll down to the bottom to find out which statues you’re breaking.
